h2. Good programming principles

h3. DRY

Don't repeat yourself.

In software engineering, Don't Repeat Yourself (DRY) or Duplication is Evil (DIE) is a principle of software development aimed at reducing repetition of information of all kinds, especially useful in multi-tier architectures. The DRY principle is stated as 

bq. "Every piece of knowledge must have a single, unambiguous, authoritative representation within a system

h3. Convention over Configuration

bq. "Convention over Configuration (also known as Coding by convention) is a software design paradigm which seeks to decrease the number of decisions that developers need to make, gaining simplicity, but not necessarily losing flexibility."

h3. KISS

Keep it simple stupid

bq. "The KISS principle states that simplicity should be a key goal, and that unnecessary complexity should be avoided."
